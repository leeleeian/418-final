Summary

We build a parallel simulation of a limit order book to study how synchronization strategies impact scalability under realistic financial workloads.

Background

A limit order book processes buy and sell orders using price-time priority. Matching operations depend on shared global state, making parallelization challenging.

The Challenge

The primary challenge in parallelizing a limit order book lies in the tension between concurrency and correctness. While naive approaches such as coarse-grained locking severely limit scalability due to contention, more sophisticated methods like fine-grained locking and lock-free data structures introduce significant overhead from synchronization, retries, and cache coherence effects. Furthermore, real-world workloads exhibit skew, with most activity concentrated near the top of the book, exacerbating contention even in fine-grained designs. Our project will explore whether restructuring the computation—through batching, agent-level parallelism, and partial partitioning—can reduce synchronization frequency and improve throughput, rather than attempting to fully parallelize inherently sequential components like order matching.
